1. Model Overview

This simulation models a supplement e-commerce business across three sales channels (DTC, Retail, Amazon) over a configurable time period. The model calculates monthly results for revenue, costs, profitability, customer metrics, inventory, and cash flow.

3. Customer Acquisition

3.1 Seasonality Adjustment

seasonalMultiplier = 1 + sin((month - 1) × π/6 + π/6) × (seasonalityFactor - 1)
Explanation: Applies seasonal variation to customer acquisition. The sine wave creates peaks in months 1-2 (January-February, New Year resolutions) and months 9-10 (September-October, back-to-school/fall fitness).

3.2 Organic Customer Acquisition

organicTraffic = organicTrafficMonthly × (1 + marketGrowthRate/100)(month-1)/12
organicCustomers = organicTraffic × (organicConversionRate/100)
Explanation: Organic traffic grows at the market growth rate compounded monthly. Customers are acquired by applying the organic conversion rate to traffic.

3.3 Paid Advertising Customers

dtcAdBudget = monthlyMarketingBudget × (dtcPaidAdSpend/100)
baseCPC = baseCostPerClick
cpcInflation = (1 + cpcAnnualInflation/100)(month-1)/12
currentCPC = baseCPC × cpcInflation
paidTraffic = dtcAdBudget / currentCPC
paidCustomers = paidTraffic × (paidAdConversionRate/100)
Explanation: CPC (cost per click) increases over time due to market maturity and competition. Budget is divided by current CPC to get traffic, then conversion rate is applied.

3.4 Influencer Marketing Customers

influencerBudget = monthlyMarketingBudget × (influencerMarketingSpend/100)
influencerCustomers = (influencerBudget / influencerCostPerEngagement) × (influencerConversionRate/100)
Explanation: Budget is divided by cost per engagement to get total engagements, then conversion rate is applied to get customers.

3.5 Referral Customers

monthlyReferralRate = customerReferralRate / 100 / 12
referralCustomers = totalCustomers × monthlyReferralRate
Explanation: Annual referral rate is converted to monthly, then applied to total customer base. This creates compound growth as customer base increases.

3.6 Total New Customers

newCustomers = (organicCustomers + paidCustomers + influencerCustomers + referralCustomers) × seasonalMultiplier

4. Customer Retention & Churn

4.1 Monthly Retention Rate

annualRetentionRate = repeatPurchaseRate / 100
monthlyRetentionRate = annualRetentionRate1/12
monthlyChurnRate = 1 - monthlyRetentionRate
Explanation: Annual retention rate is converted to monthly using compound rate formula. For example, 90% annual retention = 99.13% monthly retention (0.901/12).

4.2 Customer Base Update

churnedCustomers = totalCustomers × monthlyChurnRate
activeCustomers = totalCustomers - churnedCustomers
totalCustomers = activeCustomers + newCustomers

4.3 Purchase Frequency (Probabilistic Model)

monthlyPurchaseProbability = 1 / purchaseFrequencyMonths
repeatPurchases = activeCustomers × monthlyPurchaseProbability
purchasesThisMonth = newCustomers + repeatPurchases
Explanation: Each active customer has a probability of purchasing equal to 1/frequency. For 3-month frequency, each customer has 33.3% chance of buying this month. This is multiplied by total active customers to get expected repeat purchases.

5. Revenue Calculations

5.1 Channel Distribution

dtcPurchases = purchasesThisMonth × (dtcChannelMix/100)
retailPurchases = purchasesThisMonth × (retailChannelMix/100)
amazonPurchases = purchasesThisMonth × (amazonChannelMix/100)
Validation: dtcChannelMix + retailChannelMix + amazonChannelMix must equal 100%

5.2 Units Sold

avgUnitsPerOrder = avgUnitsPerOrder (parameter)
dtcUnitsSold = dtcPurchases × avgUnitsPerOrder
retailUnitsSold = retailPurchases × avgUnitsPerOrder
amazonUnitsSold = amazonPurchases × avgUnitsPerOrder
totalUnitsSold = dtcUnitsSold + retailUnitsSold + amazonUnitsSold

5.3 Stockout Adjustment

IF inventory < totalUnitsSold THEN:
stockoutRatio = inventory / totalUnitsSold
dtcUnitsSold = dtcUnitsSold × stockoutRatio
retailUnitsSold = retailUnitsSold × stockoutRatio
amazonUnitsSold = amazonUnitsSold × stockoutRatio
totalUnitsSold = inventory
Explanation: If inventory runs out, all channels are reduced proportionally based on available inventory. This ensures revenue calculations match actual units that can be sold.

5.4 DTC Revenue

subscriptionPortion = dtcPurchases × (subscriptionAdoptionRate/100)
regularDtcPortion = dtcPurchases - subscriptionPortion
regularRevenue = regularDtcPortion × avgSellingPriceDTC
subscriptionRevenue = subscriptionPortion × avgSellingPriceDTC × (1 - subscriptionDiscountPercentage/100)
shippingCosts = dtcPurchases × dtcShippingCost
dtcRevenue = regularRevenue + subscriptionRevenue - shippingCosts
Explanation: DTC revenue is calculated separately for regular and subscription customers. Shipping costs are per ORDER (not per unit) and deducted from gross revenue.

5.5 Retail Revenue

retailRevenue = retailUnitsSold × avgWholesalePrice
Explanation: Retail revenue is based on wholesale price (what YOU receive from retailers), not the retail shelf price.

5.6 Amazon Revenue

amazonRevenue = amazonUnitsSold × avgSellingPriceDTC × (1 - amazonFeePercentage/100)
Explanation: Amazon revenue is DTC price minus Amazon's percentage fee (includes FBA fees, referral fees, etc.).

5.7 Total Revenue

totalRevenue = dtcRevenue + retailRevenue + amazonRevenue

6. Cost Calculations

6.1 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

COGS = totalUnitsSold × avgManufacturingCostPerUnit
Explanation: Direct cost of units sold. Based on accrual accounting (recognized when sold, not when manufactured).

6.2 Manufacturing & QA Costs

QA for sold units = ⌈totalUnitsSold / unitsPerBatch⌉ × qualityTestingCost
QA for new inventory = ⌈reorderedUnits / unitsPerBatch⌉ × qualityTestingCost
manufacturingCosts = QA for sold units + QA for new inventory
Explanation: QA testing is performed per batch. Costs are incurred both for current sales and for new inventory purchases. Ceiling function (⌈⌉) rounds up partial batches.

6.3 Marketing Costs

marketingCosts = monthlyMarketingBudget
Explanation: Total marketing budget (already allocated to DTC paid ads, influencer marketing, and retail trade marketing).

6.4 Fulfillment Costs

dtcFulfillment = (dtcUnitsSold + amazonUnitsSold) × fulfillmentCostDTC
retailFulfillment = retailUnitsSold × fulfillmentCostRetail
fulfillmentCosts = dtcFulfillment + retailFulfillment
Explanation: DTC and Amazon use same fulfillment cost (pick, pack, ship individual orders). Retail has lower cost (bulk shipments to distributors).

6.5 Operational Costs

rdCosts = totalRevenue × (rdExpensePercentage/100)
warehousingCosts = totalUnitsSold × warehouseCostPerUnit
paymentProcessing = totalRevenue × (paymentProcessingFee/100)
operationalCosts = monthlyFixedCosts + insuranceAndLegalCosts + warehousingCosts + paymentProcessing + rdCosts
Explanation: Operational costs include fixed overhead plus variable costs (warehousing, payment processing, R&D).

6.6 Total Costs

totalCosts = COGS + manufacturingCosts + marketingCosts + fulfillmentCosts + operationalCosts

7. Profitability Metrics

7.1 Gross Profit

grossProfit = totalRevenue - COGS - fulfillmentCosts
grossMargin = (grossProfit / totalRevenue) × 100
Explanation: Gross profit follows standard accounting: revenue minus direct costs (COGS + fulfillment). Excludes marketing and overhead.

7.2 Net Profit

netProfit = totalRevenue - totalCosts
netMargin = (netProfit / totalRevenue) × 100
Explanation: Net profit is revenue minus all costs (COGS, manufacturing, marketing, fulfillment, operational).

8. Inventory Management

8.1 Inventory Depletion

inventory = inventory - totalUnitsSold
monthlyExpirationRate = (expirationWastePercentage/100) / 12
shelfLifeAdjustment = 24 / productShelfLife
adjustedExpirationRate = monthlyExpirationRate × shelfLifeAdjustment
expirationWaste = inventory × adjustedExpirationRate
inventory = inventory - expirationWaste
Explanation: Inventory decreases from sales and expiration. Expiration rate adjusts based on shelf life (shorter shelf life = higher monthly waste).

8.2 Dynamic Inventory Parameters

Months 1-3: Gradual Transition

IF salesHistory.length = 0 THEN:
dynamicMinInventory = minimumInventoryLevel
dynamicReorderQuantity = reorderQuantity
ELSE IF salesHistory.length < 3 THEN:
weight = salesHistory.length / 3
avgActualSales = average(salesHistory)
dynamicMin = avgActualSales × 1.5
dynamicReorder = avgActualSales × 3
dynamicMinInventory = (minimumInventoryLevel × (1 - weight)) + (dynamicMin × weight)
dynamicReorderQuantity = (reorderQuantity × (1 - weight)) + (dynamicReorder × weight)
Explanation: First 3 months gradually blend static parameters with actual sales data. Weight increases from 33% (month 2) to 67% (month 3) to 100% (month 4+).

Month 4+: Fully Dynamic

avgMonthlySales = average(salesHistory)
firstHalfAvg = average(first half of salesHistory)
secondHalfAvg = average(second half of salesHistory)
growthRate = min((secondHalfAvg - firstHalfAvg) / firstHalfAvg, 0.5)
projectedMonthlySales = avgMonthlySales × (1 + growthRate)
dynamicMinInventory = projectedMonthlySales × 1.5
dynamicReorderQuantity = projectedMonthlySales × 3
Explanation: After month 3, inventory levels are based on rolling sales history with growth trend analysis. Growth rate is capped at 50% for safety. Maintains 1.5 months safety stock and reorders 3 months supply.

8.3 Reorder Logic

IF inventory < dynamicMinInventory THEN:
unitCost = avgManufacturingCostPerUnit
qaCostPerUnit = qualityTestingCost / unitsPerBatch
totalUnitCost = unitCost + qaCostPerUnit
reorderCost = dynamicReorderQuantity × totalUnitCost
IF availableCash ≥ reorderCost THEN:
inventory += dynamicReorderQuantity (full reorder)
ELSE IF availableCash ≥ reorderCost × 0.3 THEN:
partialQuantity = floor(availableCash × 0.9 / totalUnitCost)
inventory += partialQuantity (partial reorder)
ELSE:
No reorder (insufficient cash)
Explanation: When inventory drops below minimum, attempt full reorder if cash available. If cash-constrained but at least 30% of needed amount available, do partial reorder using 90% of available cash. Otherwise skip reorder.

9. Cash Flow

9.1 Operating Cash Flow

cashOperatingExpenses = manufacturingCosts + marketingCosts + fulfillmentCosts + operationalCosts
operatingCashFlow = totalRevenue - cashOperatingExpenses
Explanation: Operating cash flow excludes inventory purchases (separate cash use). Note that COGS is NOT included here to avoid double-counting with inventory purchases.
IMPORTANT: COGS is accrual-based (expense when sold). Inventory purchases are cash-based (expense when bought). Including both would double-count inventory costs.

9.2 Net Cash Flow

netCashFlow = operatingCashFlow - inventoryPurchaseCost
cumulativeCashFlow = cumulativeCashFlow + netCashFlow
Explanation: Net cash flow is operating cash flow minus inventory purchases. Cumulative cash flow tracks running cash balance.

9.3 Working Capital

inventoryValue = inventory × avgManufacturingCostPerUnit
receivables = totalRevenue × 0.1
workingCapital = inventoryValue + receivables
Explanation: Working capital is simplified to inventory value plus estimated receivables (10% of monthly revenue). Does not include accounts payable.

10. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

10.1 LTV Calculation Methodology

annualRetentionRate = repeatPurchaseRate / 100
churnRate = 1 - annualRetentionRate
theoreticalLifespanYears = 1 / churnRate
avgCustomerLifespanYears = min(theoreticalLifespanYears, maxCustomerLifespanYears)
Explanation: Customer lifespan is inverse of churn rate, capped at maximum realistic value. For example, 10% churn = 10 year theoretical lifespan, capped at 2.5 years.

10.2 Purchase Frequency

purchaseFrequencyPerYear = min(12 / purchaseFrequencyMonths, maxPurchasesPerYear)
lifetimePurchases = avgCustomerLifespanYears × purchaseFrequencyPerYear
Explanation: Annual purchase frequency is capped at maximum realistic value. Total lifetime purchases = years × purchases per year.

10.3 Weighted Average Order Value

subscriptionRate = subscriptionAdoptionRate / 100
subscriptionDiscount = subscriptionDiscountPercentage / 100
regularAOV = avgOrderValue × (1 - subscriptionRate)
subscriptionAOV = avgOrderValue × (1 - subscriptionDiscount) × subscriptionRate
weightedAOV = regularAOV + subscriptionAOV
Explanation: Blends regular and subscription order values based on adoption rate. Subscription customers pay discounted price.

10.4 Final LTV Calculation

calculatedLTV = weightedAOV × lifetimePurchases
finalLTV = calculatedLTV × ltvConservativeAdjustment
Explanation: LTV is order value × lifetime purchases, then adjusted conservatively (typically 75% of theoretical value) to account for real-world churn variations.

11. Key Performance Metrics

11.1 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC = marketingCosts / newCustomers

11.2 Average Order Value (AOV)

totalDirectOrders = dtcPurchases + amazonPurchases
totalDirectRevenue = dtcRevenue + amazonRevenue
AOV = totalDirectRevenue / totalDirectOrders
Explanation: AOV is calculated only for direct channels (DTC + Amazon) since retail uses wholesale pricing.

11.3 Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)

ROAS = totalRevenue / marketingCosts

11.4 Inventory Turnover

beginningInventory = inventory + totalUnitsSold
avgInventory = (beginningInventory + inventory) / 2
inventoryTurnover = (totalUnitsSold / avgInventory) × 12
Explanation: Annualized inventory turnover rate. Shows how many times per year inventory is sold and replaced.

11.5 LTV:CAC Ratio

LTV:CAC = customerLifetimeValue / customerAcquisitionCost
Explanation: Key profitability metric. Healthy businesses typically have LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 or higher.
